Research

I needed to understand what was already on the market. By understanding what was already being offered to consumers, I would better understand how to help those consumers reach their goals.

I did so by conducting a competitive analysis on two apps of the most popular apps in the market and this research was essential in my design process. I performed content audits, google surveys, and a competitive analysis. I came away from this with a few things in mind:

  1. Users love the real-time weather map this is provided by windy.app.
  2. A visual ofÂ the beach is essential for users to feel connected to the sea and the community.
  3. No one wants clutter!
  4. The demographic for this app does not want to spend more than 5 minutes on their phone.

More User Research

With my mid-fidelity wireframes done, I needed to do another round of user testing. I contacted 5 potential users (ages 24-35) for moderated in-person tests. I came away from this with multiple revisions in mind. Some of the most pressing revisions are:

  1. I needed to change the layout of the weather reports page. There was no need to have an extra screen before the information was displayed. The point of this app is to have users be quick!
  2. Participants had trouble finding the button to change the filters on the weatherÂ map. Using a different icon and some color will help users.
  3. Finding the new post button on the social page was challengingÂ for participants.
